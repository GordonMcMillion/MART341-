How to tie a Fly


Yellow mayfly pattern

  • Hook: Mustad R30
  • Thread: Sheer 14/0 Yellow
  • Tail: Ginger Coq De Leon
  • Wings: Picric dyed Mallard flank
  • 1/2Body: Yellow turkey biot
  • Hackle: Whiting grizzle saddle dyed yellow

Directions

  1. 1. Secure your dry fly hook in the vice, as shown, with the hook shank horizontal.
  2. 2. Run a slim foundation of tying thread along the hook shank, as shown.
  3. 3. Select a nicely marked ginger Coq de Leon hackle for the tail fibres. Tie in the bunch on top of the hook shank. The tail should be approximately the same length as the hook shank.
  4. 4. Select a mallard flank feather with long barbules and nice markings. Make sure that there are no short barbules in the two sections to be used for the wings.
  5. 5. Strip off the down fibres at the base of the hackle. Wet the barbules at the tip of the flank feather and straighten.
  6. 6. Repeat to make the second wing.
  7. 7. Trim off the excess hackle and whip finish.
  8. 8. Apply a drop of varnish to the head and your yellow may is ready.
  9. 9. all done!
